# 📊 Trader Performance vs Market Sentiment Analysis

## 📌 Objective
Analyze how market sentiment (Fear vs Greed) impacts trader behavior and profitability using real trading data.

---

## 📂 Dataset
- Historical Trader Data (Hyperliquid)
- Bitcoin Fear & Greed Index

---

## ⚙️ Methodology
- Data cleaning and preprocessing  
- Timestamp conversion and date alignment  
- Merging trading data with sentiment data  
- Feature engineering (PnL, win/loss, trade size)  
- Sentiment grouping (Fear, Greed, Neutral)  
- Exploratory Data Analysis using boxplots  

---

## 📈 Key Insights

- Traders perform better during **Greed periods** with higher average PnL  
- **Fear periods** show higher losses and lower win probability  
- **Large trades generate significantly higher returns** than small trades  
- Neutral markets show relatively stable but moderate performance  
- Losses dominate during Fear sentiment, indicating higher risk conditions  

---

## 🚀 Strategy Recommendations

- Reduce exposure and avoid aggressive trading during **Fear periods**  
- Trade actively during **Greed periods** with proper risk management  
- Focus on **high-quality trades with larger position sizes**  
- Adapt trading strategy based on market sentiment  

---

## 📊 Tools & Technologies
- Python (Pandas, NumPy)  
- Data Visualization (Seaborn, Matplotlib)  
- Jupyter Notebook  

---

## 🧠 Conclusion
Market sentiment has a strong influence on trader performance and risk behavior.  
Sentiment-aware strategies can significantly improve profitability and risk control.
